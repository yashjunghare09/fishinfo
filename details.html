Salmon

Characteristics

Salmon are known for their bright pink flesh and distinctive flavor. They can weigh up to 100 pounds and have a streamlined body, making them excellent swimmers.

Diet

Salmon are carnivorous and primarily eat smaller fish, invertebrates, and zooplankton. Their diet varies based on their age and the environment they inhabit.

Habitat

Salmon are anadromous, meaning they are born in freshwater, migrate to the ocean, and return to freshwater to spawn. They thrive in clean, cold rivers and lakes.

Conservation Status

Many salmon populations are threatened by overfishing, habitat loss, and pollution. Conservation efforts include habitat restoration and sustainable fishing practices.

Interesting Facts

  • Salmon can leap up to 12 feet when swimming upstream.
  • They can recognize their home stream by smell.
  • Different salmon species have varying lifespans, with some living only a few years, while others can live up to seven years.
